Grouple

2014-Present

Social app for getting together with friends and participating in events. When you propose an event in Grouple, you don't invite friends, you invite your groups. As development continues users will be able to do things like pull up a map of their local area and see all public events going on and click to be brought directly to the event profile and get in on the action. Grouple is for promoting socialization outside of the realm of media. Options like minimum and maximum participants, recurring events, automating the rescheduling of events to better fit all potential event attendees, and much more make Grouple unique. Users earn badges and levels while using the app, which serves as an extra incentive to get out and active in their community. Event messaging, group messaging and user messaging keep communication organized and to the point.

Grouple also sports a settings panel that lets you enable or disable both push notifications and email notifications for everything that happens within the app to help keep you in the know to what is going on.

Android version was made as part of my senior project, I led a team of 3, we practiced agile (SCRUM). Images below are from our android version we presented in May 2015. All of the icons were created in house using Illustrator.

Currently working on recreating the front-end / back-end of the application via javascript. I will be packaging it together using Phonegap for all devices.

Technologies used: AngularJS, NodeJS, MySQL DB, Browserify, Cordova, Karma-Jasmine, Gulp

GitHub is host to all of the Android and Javascript version code. You can go directly to that here.

QuickPrint

Turns an ordinary Xerox scanner into a walk-up kiosk mode for customers. Customers walk up, are instructed through a login process, a session is started. Meter information is grabbed behind the scenes and upon ending finishing working with the copier, a receipt is calculated and displayed through the app. Then the customer prints off the receipt and brings it up to the cashier to finish their transaction.

Disc Golf Website

2014

Created an interactive disc golf website where users could register, login and record their rounds at various disc golf parks that were put into the server. Features were: course standings where you could sort by a multitude of statistics, and personal statistics page breaking down your skills over time. I also connected the site to an open source php forum for the community to use for meet ups at local courses, or just talk about disc golf in general.
